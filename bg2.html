English Grammar

English grammar is the foundation of clear communication. Understanding its rules helps in effective writing and speaking.

1. Parts of Speech

English consists of eight parts of speech:

  • Noun - Names a person, place, or thing. (e.g., cat, London)
  • Pronoun - Replaces a noun. (e.g., he, she, they)
  • Verb - Indicates action or state. (e.g., run, is)
  • Adjective - Describes a noun. (e.g., blue, happy)
  • Adverb - Modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb. (e.g., quickly, very)
  • Preposition - Shows relation between words. (e.g., on, in)
  • Conjunction - Connects phrases. (e.g., and, but)
  • Interjection - Expresses emotion. (e.g., wow!, ouch!)

2. Sentence Structure

English follows a subject-verb-object (SVO) structure.

Example: She (subject) loves (verb) coffee (object).

More on: Sentence Structure.

3. Tenses in English

The three primary tenses:

  • Past: She walked to school.
  • Present: She walks to school.
  • Future: She will walk to school.
